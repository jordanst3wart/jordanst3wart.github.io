Jordan Hall

writes things

I write about staying alive, paying attention, and the quiet terror of being understood. Essays, fragments, and the occasional photograph. Based somewhere between the coast and the mountains.

I believe in sentences that breathe, in work that takes the time it takes, and in keeping at least one window open — even in winter.

Currently working on a collection of essays about thresholds, doorways, and the spaces we hesitate in.
